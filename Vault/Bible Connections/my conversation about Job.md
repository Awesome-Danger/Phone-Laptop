I just had a conversation about Job with someone who had read the book last week for her Bible class, but didn't really understand it. She doesn't know English very well, and isn't super familiar with the Bible, but growing up in the Church, I am.

So naturally, I talked in depth about the overall story of Job, his friends, his friends' and own attitudes, and finally God's response. I told her about the English meanings of sorrow, mourning, and sacrifice, telling her about how sacrifices were made, and how that connects to Jesus' sacrifice for us. She found all of this information very interesting, and after this, I had been reminded of just how much I love talking about God, His son, His Spirit, their will, and our response.

It also reminded me of how many people really don't know about what Jesus *really* means; how much of the Bible people haven't actually read.
