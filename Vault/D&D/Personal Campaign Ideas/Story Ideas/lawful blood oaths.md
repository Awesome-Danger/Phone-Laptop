A blood oath is an unbreakable promise made between two people. The one giving the promised must forge and sharpen, or otherwise provide the weapon that they would will be killed with by the recipient if they fail. The oath maker may choose how they will be killed if the oath is broken. The blood oath is sealed by engraving a symbol in the blade and cutting both participants palms, then clasping/pressing their palms together. 
A blood oath is respected by law. If the Oath Maker becomes the Oath Breaker and must die, and the oath breaker must submit to their death by the recipient. Failure to do so may result in an order to kill on site, or a branding with the oath breaker symbol and banishment. Failure to comply with banishment will result in a manhunt and a warrant to kill on sight.
