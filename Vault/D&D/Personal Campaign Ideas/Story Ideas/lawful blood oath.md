- an unbreakable promise made between two people. 
- the one giving the promise must forge and sharpen, or otherwise provide a weapon they will be killed with by the recipient if they fail. The oath maker may choose how they will be killed if the oath is broken (if still honorable). 
- The blood oath is sealed by engraving the symbol of the blood oath in the blade and cutting both participants palms, then clasping/pressing their palms together. 
A blood oath is respected by law. If the Oath Maker becomes the Oath Breaker, he must die, and submit to their death by the recipient. Failure to do so may result in an order to kill on site, or a branding with the Oath Breaker symbol, followed by banishment. 
- Failure to comply with banishment will result in a manhunt and an order to kill on sight.

### why make an oath?
1. depending on the nature of the oath, you can gain certain benefits related to completing the oath, because the level of dedication you have shown proves that you can be trusted.
	- people may open their doors to you, or even be required to by law
	- unlimited access to certain areas, and/or resources
2. after completing an oath, you have proven that you can be trusted in general, which can be either a mark of honor/pride to a regular person OR
3. a redeeming action of a prisoner to regain the trust of a community, though it may not be as effective as one would hope (though if you honestly completed several difficult oaths of this nature, just about anyone would trust you, no matter who you are).
4. You can also be charged with the option of an oath, with the promise of a reward from the one who made the charge. The reward can be bargained for
	- reward can be monetary (if that proves to be honorable/of great importance to the Oath Keeper)
	- usually a promise of land, or personal benefits, such as an inheritance; being accepted as a part of the family forever, helping to unite families or lovers (king accepts you into the royal family, father lets you marry his daughter, and gives a dowry/inheritance, etc.); guarantee of safety, immunity, shelter/home, aid when given; and, though rare, a debt to be collected at an undisclosed time.
