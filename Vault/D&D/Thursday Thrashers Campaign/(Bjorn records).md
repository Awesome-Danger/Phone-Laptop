## Bjorn The Dragonborn
Age: 26
Height: 6’ 9”
Weight 320 lbs
Race: Dragonborn
Scale color: Brass
Class: Druid, [Creed Bounty Hunter]
Alignment: Chaotic Good
Attributes: Nose blind, Often overlooks his own safety, Enjoys the act of combat, but prefers to avoid it
Bonds: Oath of the Creed + Nomad,

## Background
My name is Bjorn. When I was an egg, I was taken away from my parents by a creed known as the creed of Belgabad; a secret creed that takes Dragonborn hatchlings, and trains them to be prodigious killing machines. Because they took me away, I don’t know who my parents are, or if they’re even still alive. I wasn’t alone in this however, because I was just 1 out of 7 eggs total that were stolen from other Dragonborn families to be part of a brotherhood in the creed. From the time we were 1 year old, we were trained how to fight, use weapons and armor, life skills, intimidation, and how to work with the brotherhood. My brotherhood was monitored, controlled, and authorized by the creed. We feared and respected the creed for all it was; we never asked questions, never challenged authority, and never betrayed the creed. It’s a tough life at the creed. Every day we trained, and sparred with each other to maintain our skills, and to not grow soft. My creed brothers and I grew up with only each other, and our masters and superiors. We didn’t come into contact with the rest of the creed very often, but it kept us in our place to know of the other brotherhoods under the creed that would be sent to kill us without hesitation if we went too out of line. We only saw other parts of the creed a couple of times. One of the most important was for us to be given our weapons; the other was when we made our oaths. My brothers and I had 2 teachers. One for life and survival, and one for combat. Our life teacher, who had silver scales was Teacher Uromorn; we called him Dogma because he was a very wise teacher, and everything he taught to us was proved to be absolutely true. Our combat teacher, who had bronze scales was Teacher Helazar; we called him Din both because he always made us repeat everything more times than we can count, and because of his roar if we got too rowdy. It also became fitting later…
My brothers and I all took our oaths to the creed when we entered adulthood at 16. The day we gave our oaths, we went to a part of the crags we had never been before on top of all of the mountains in the middle of the darkest night. Our teachers had prepared us for this day, so we were looking forward to it, and were excited. That all vanished when we saw Belgabad. He was a massive copper dragon that was only a silhouette in the distance with his wings partially spread out. When we beheld the massive creature in front of us, we all were struck with an incredible fear. The giant silhouette started to move as if to walk towards us, but then started to shrink. As he approached, he shrank and shrank until he became about the same height as us in the form of a stag with larger antlers. As his silhouette left the shadows and entered the light, his face looked only 
Each of us were had a choice between a few creed crafted weapons and/or given our own specialized gear that the creed made just for us. The weapons the creed gave to us are these: Brother Argetak/Dancer was given a silver double-pronged double bladed pole arm because of the color of his scales, and his unique fighting style and abilities, which involved a lot stabbing and spinning. Brother Karrtok/Cleaver, who had bronze scales, was given a greatsword because he loved to slash with heavy blows and didn’t want to hold a shield, off-hand/parrying weapon, or anything of the like. Brother Azarax/Shusher, who had blue scales, was given a short sword and shield because of his balance between defense and offense. Brother Dramorn/Flourisher, who had black scales, was given a rokushakubo (long staff) because of his personality, discipline and resourcefulness. Brother Arroth/Staffbiter, who had gold scales, was given a whip because of his ruthlessness, and finesse. Brother Navroth/Shieldsmasher, who had green scales, was given a large maul because he loved to smash and break bones and shields with brute force. Then finally, there was me: Brother Bjorn/Savage, I have brass scales, and I was given 2 hand sized sickles, because I loved to (and sometimes still do) slash and rip open my prey and eat their meat.
We were all given other secondary weapons, ranged or mele, as well. All of us were given a hidden retractable blade on one of our arms, and we all had cestuses as well. As for our secondary weapons, we were allowed to choose ours. I chose a hand crossbow that was modified to fit on my arm like my hidden dagger. I wanted this because I found that it would be useful for hunting, and for a hidden surprise.

The creed sent me and my 6 other creed brothers when we were 16 on a mission to burn and destroy a village. Our target was Wu Shang. We were told that if we wreaked havoc on the village and all its inhabitants, we would likely destroy the target. The creed led us the drop-off point by having us ride giant flying creatures. We rode for several days and stopped only to feed and water the creatures while we had our daily sparring match in the morning. We didn’t stop for anything else. We ate and drank on their backs, and even peed off the side of them, as to not waste any time. They had a rendezvous point set up at the foot of the mountains, for after we completed our mission. We had 7 days to meet at their rendezvous point, or we would be presumed dead. We arrived at the drop-off and had to take a half-day’s journey on foot to the village. After the creed members left, my brothers and I wanted to split up to cover more ground and meet at our own rendezvous point before going to the creed’s rendezvous. We went to the village, and we surrounded the village on all sides, and attacked before they knew what hit them. We had already burned several houses when we started killing many of the villagers. I and 2 of my other brothers teamed up together to round up and slaughter all the pregnant women, so that we could draw out the bounty. 
Around the middle of the day, we saw a villager covered in blood approaching, and with a limp on his right leg. When we saw this villager, he looked like most of them, except he had a burning fire in his eyes not unlike the fire from my own maw. He stood there, staff in hand and covered in blood from himself, and from my brothers. I, brother Dramorn/Shusher and Azarax/Flourisher attempted to fight him. Shusher and I tried to flank him while Flourisher tried to “test” him head on, because he too had a staff. This villager had incredible skill. I had never seen anyone able to meet Flourisher’s skill with a staff, so I was, and still am, a little impressed. This villager survived my 4 other brothers and was putting up a good fight with the 3 of us. This must have been Wu Shang.
When he started to fail and took a slash from me, and a shield bash from Shusher, and his bleeding and exhaustion after about a minute of fighting started to get the better of him, more villagers with weapons started to surround us on all sides. We realized that we were outnumbered, so the 3 of us used our breaths of acid, lightning and fire to keep the villagers away, and make a path for us to escape before we were killed. On the first breath attack, my breath hit the villager we had been fighting in the leg that he was bleeding out of, while the 3 of us were back-to-back-to-back. That was to keep the villagers back, but the second time, we all faced one direction to combine our breath attacks to make the villagers dive out of the way, making a path of escape for us. The villager we had been fighting was on the ground, screaming in pain while trying to put the fire on his leg out. My brothers and I fled and escaped into the mountains.
On our way out, I met a mother who was holding her newborn child. We weren’t to allow her to live, so I killed the mother by shooting her between the eyes with my hidden crossbow. As I was going to do the same with the baby, I looked into her eyes, and didn’t see a normal infant. The child didn’t cry, but instead, when she had rolled over, looked at me with fire in her eyes. Not like the fire of my kin’s deathbringer, not like the fire in my breast, but a cold fire. She might not be able to breathe fire like us, but those eyes weren’t the eyes of a baby, they were the eyes of a woman who wanted vengeance for her mother. I knew it may be a mistake, but I took her with us to raise her as one of our own, even if she were to kill me after finding out who killed her mother.
When the 3 of us made it to the rendezvous point our brotherhood had set (not the creed), we noticed that our other brothers had not shown up. We rested there for a few hours but couldn’t stay for very long because of the mob that would come after us. When our other brothers still didn’t show up, we hoped that we would see our brothers at the rendezvous point the creed had set for us. We rode on wild horses that Flourisher tamed for us to the rendezvous point. After several hours, we arrived and saw that our other brothers never arrived. Still, we waited until the creed came to pick us up, but they never came. When the creed finally came, and picked us up, we finally mourned their deaths. 
Because the creed made us a group of 7 brothers from birth, the creed knew that we would soon become mad if we stayed in our “brotherhood” against our will, so they gave the 3 of us a few options. We could become teachers of certain skills within the creed, we could we could become additions to another brotherhood (which they knew was highly unlikely), we could leave the creed headquarters and become nomadic creed members that help the creed when in time of need, we could be breeders for new hatchlings, or our as our last option, we could fight a leader of the creed to the death to gain a mark of honor and leave the creed. 
Shusher/Azarax tried to fight a teacher of the creed to the death. However, the custom of the creed is to fight your teacher of combat if they are still alive, and ours was. Azarax tried to fight Helazar and won. Because I was there, I saw Azarax drive his short-sword through Helazar’s stomach, and I watched our teacher collapse to the ground. I saw Helazar slightly move his lips toward Azarax’s ear, but what he said, if anything, was completely inaudible. Azarax got the mark of honor, and used it to permanently leave the creed. He left before I could even say goodbye. 
Flourisher/Dramorn became a teacher of discipline and strategy, and continues to work in the creed today.
As for myself, I became a nomadic creed member, and now roam the world for another purpose in life. The creed is asking for help when I'm told the phrase "The Crags of Belgabad are calling." This means that I have to give aid in whatever way they ask, whether that's clearing the way for a brotherhood, giving a brotherhood mounts and supplies, or having to go to the Crags of Belgabad and give aid in whatever way I can there. My life is in the hands of the creed. It can also work the other way around. If I tell a creed messenger “Your brother is in need.” I can ask for supplies, or a mount or something simple, but handy. I can ask for a brotherhood to come to my aid if the need is dire, but I cannot however, ask for a large army, or the entire creed to come to my aid, unless the threat puts the creed itself in immediate and incredible danger.

Sometimes he will wear his bear hide cloak instead. He has a wide uneven gash on his face across his nose.

I couldn’t smell after that. However, the memory of smell will sometimes trick me into thinking that I smell blood occasionally. My nose blindness has caused my sense of taste to be slightly muted, but only slightly. I did make a good cloak out of that bear's hide though. I didn't keep the head on my cloak, because I prefer not to wear hoods, but I won't say no to wearing one.
